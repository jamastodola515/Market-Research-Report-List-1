<p><p>The automotive buffers market comprises two primary types: electrical buffers and pneumatic buffers. Electrical buffers utilize electric mechanisms to absorb shocks and vibrations, offering precise control and responsiveness for vehicle performance. They are often employed in advanced suspension systems. Pneumatic buffers, on the other hand, rely on compressed air to provide cushioning and support, making them ideal for heavy-duty applications. Both types enhance ride quality, improve safety, and contribute to better overall vehicle dynamics in various automotive contexts.</p></p>
