<p><p>Crystalline ceramics are advanced materials with ordered atomic structures, widely used in electronics, optics, and energy applications. **Monocrystalline ceramics** consist of a single crystal lattice, offering superior mechanical, thermal, and electrical properties, ideal for high-performance applications like semiconductors. **Polycrystalline ceramics** are composed of multiple crystal grains, providing balanced properties and cost-effectiveness, commonly used in sensors and insulators. **Others** include amorphous or mixed-phase ceramics, tailored for specific functionalities like thermal insulation or biocompatibility, catering to niche industrial and medical applications. Each type serves distinct market needs based on performance and cost.</p></p>
