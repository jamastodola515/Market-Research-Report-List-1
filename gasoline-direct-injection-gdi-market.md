<p><p>Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI) is a fuel delivery system where gasoline is injected directly into the combustion chamber, improving efficiency and performance. In the 4-stroke GDI market, engines complete intake, compression, power, and exhaust strokes, offering better fuel economy and lower emissions, making them popular in modern vehicles. The 2-stroke GDI market, though less common, combines intake and compression in one stroke and power and exhaust in another, providing higher power-to-weight ratios but with higher emissions, often used in smaller engines like motorcycles or tools.</p></p>
