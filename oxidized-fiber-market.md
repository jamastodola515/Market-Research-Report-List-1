<p><p>The oxidized fiber market is segmented into two main types: continuous fiber and non-continuous fiber. Continuous fiber involves long strands that provide enhanced strength and flexibility, making them suitable for applications such as aerospace and automotive components. In contrast, non-continuous fiber consists of shorter strands, typically used in less demanding applications like insulation and composites. Each type serves distinct purposes, catering to different industrial needs, with continuous fibers often favored for high-performance environments and non-continuous fibers for cost-effective solutions.</p></p>
