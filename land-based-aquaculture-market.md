<p><p>Land-based aquaculture refers to the practice of farming aquatic organisms such as fish, shellfish, and plants in controlled, land-based systems like tanks, ponds, or recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS). Unlike traditional open-water aquaculture, land-based systems offer greater control over environmental conditions, reducing risks associated with pollution, disease, and climate change. This method is gaining traction due to its sustainability, ability to produce high-quality seafood, and reduced environmental impact.</p><p>The Land-based Aquaculture Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for seafood, declining wild fish stocks, and advancements in aquaculture technologies. Additionally, there is a growing focus on producing species like salmon, shrimp, and tilapia, which are in high demand globally.</p><p>Sustainability and traceability are also emerging as critical factors, with consumers and regulators pushing for eco-friendly practices. The market is further supported by investments in research and development to improve feed efficiency and disease management.
