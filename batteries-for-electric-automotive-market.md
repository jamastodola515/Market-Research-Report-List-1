<p><p>The electric automotive battery market primarily consists of three types: Li-ion, NiMH, and lead-acid batteries. Li-ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density, lightweight nature, and longer lifespan, making them ideal for modern electric vehicles. NiMH batteries, while less efficient, are still used in hybrid vehicles for their reliability and cost-effectiveness. Lead-acid batteries, known for their lower cost and recycling capabilities, are mainly found in entry-level electric vehicles and starter applications, though they are less favored for long-range solutions.</p></p>
